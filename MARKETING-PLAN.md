# Marketing & Distribution Plan (25 of 105 points — do not skip this)

Most competitors will treat this as build-and-forget and score near zero here. This is your biggest
edge: a full quarter of the score, on the axis you're already good at. Run this in parallel with the build.

## 1. The demo video (your wheelhouse — this carries the axis)
A 60–90s vertical video, hook-first, in your short-form style:
- Hook (first 3s): the pain. "Ever been in a savings circle where the organizer vanished with everyone's money?"
- Problem (10s): informal thrift circles run on trust — lost records, disputes, theft.
- The fix (20s): show the app — create a circle, members contribute, the ledger everyone can see.
- Money shot (15s): a full round completing — the pot auto-releases to whose turn it is, no organizer.
- Close (5s): "Your circle. Same trust. Now on rails." + where to try it.
Keep it real and human; a genuine problem told well beats a polished-but-hollow product tour.

## 2. Real traction (this is your unfair advantage over technical competitors)
The submission goes public at the start of Week 3 for early-access testing — use that window.
- Get an actual small circle of people you know to run one real round. Real users testing a real
  savings tool is the strongest possible signal on this axis — not just a video, actual usage.
- Screenshot/record real people using it; that footage goes into the video and the description.

## 3. Community presence (cheap, direct points most people skip)
- Show up to the remaining Sip & Ship calls: July 21 and 28, 1:00–2:30 PM EST. Demo there.
- Post in the Skool community; reply to Nimiq's "shill your Mini App" thread with a crisp one-liner
  and a clip. Nimiq has said outright that marketing is scored — take them at their word.
- Post progress on X: a build-in-public thread (problem -> build -> real circle using it) is itself
  marketing and gives judges a story to follow.

## 4. The 250-word submission description (map it to the rubric)
Structure it so a judge can score you fast:
- One line on the problem (real, relatable).
- What it does + who it's for (people who use ajo/esusu/tanda/susu — millions, underserved).
- How it uses Nimiq Pay (NIM + USDT as the core UX, not decoration).
- What's newly built this cycle.
- Traction: the real circle that used it, the video, community engagement.
- Links: live mini app, public GitHub, demo video.

## Cadence for the sprint
- Day 1: register, join Skool, claim testnet NIM, start build-in-public thread.
- Day 2–3: spike done; post the first working-payment clip.
- Mid-sprint: get a real circle to test; capture footage; attend a Sip & Ship call.
- Final days: cut the demo video, write the 250-word description, submit early enough to catch the
  Week-3 early-access testing window.
